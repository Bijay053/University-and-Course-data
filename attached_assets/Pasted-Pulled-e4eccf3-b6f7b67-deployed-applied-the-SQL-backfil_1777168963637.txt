EVERY ASA course (UG and PG) hits the same path:
1. Per-course browser fetch times out at 20s
2. No per-course HTML data → no per-course English scores extracted
3. AI fallback is invoked with `missing: ielts_overall, ...`
4. AI returns SOMETHING for IELTS

That AI "something" appears to be 6.0 for every course regardless of degree level. Either:
- **(a)** AI is being given the central English page as context (which only renders Bachelor's scores), and AI applies that uniformly without checking course level, OR
- **(b)** Some other layer (maybe a fallback/default English block) writes IELTS=6.0 / PTE=50 / TOEFL=60 / CAE=169 across the board, OR  
- **(c)** AI is being prompted with a generic "ASA international requirement" string that's level-agnostic

The consistency of the values (every course gets EXACTLY 6.0/50/60/169 — no variance, even on Master's where the extracted course context differs) strongly suggests this is hardcoded fallback or a level-blind central prompt, not actual AI extraction.

## What I want you to investigate

1. **Trace the AI fallback path for English scores.** In the per-course pipeline, when the browser times out and AI enriching kicks in, what English-test data does the AI prompt include? Is the central English page text being passed in as context? If yes, that's the contamination source — it's level-agnostic by construction.

2. **Check if there's a fallback function that writes IELTS=6.0 / PTE=50 / TOEFL=60 / CAE=169 when no per-course data is found.** The numbers are too consistent across UG and PG to be AI extraction. Smells like a default block.

3. **Per-course AI prompt audit.** When the AI is asked for `missing: ielts_overall`, is the course's `degree_level` (or course name) included in the prompt? If the AI sees "extract IELTS for this URL" without level context, AND the only IELTS info available is the central page (Bachelor's), it'll always return 6.0.

## What needs to fix this

The sibling cache fix you shipped was correct in principle — it just doesn't activate when sibling cache isn't reached. Consider:

- **Level-aware AI fallback prompt** — when AI is filling a Master's course, include `degree_level=Master's` in the prompt and explicitly instruct it not to use Bachelor's-level scores from any context source
