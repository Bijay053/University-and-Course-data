ASA's central English requirements page: http://asahe.edu.au/policies-and-forms

The page renders TWO tables (visible in browser, NOT in static HTML):

## Hypothesis

Vision OCR runs against per-course page screenshots. When central page fetch FAILED, vision was given fresh screenshots that included the JS-rendered English block. When central page fetch SUCCEEDS, perhaps:
- Screenshot timing is faster (less wait time before screenshot)
- The English block hasn't rendered when the screenshot is taken
- A different page region is being captured
- Browser context is reused/cached differently

Something about the page-load -> screenshot -> OCR pipeline is timing-sensitive on ASA.
