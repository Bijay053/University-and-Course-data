The 4accb5e fix added "may not be available to international students" as a domestic-only signal. The phrase appears on UTAS course chrome for MANY courses — including ones that ARE available internationally at specific campuses. The phrase functions as a soft warning ("check your campus") rather than a hard exclusion ("never available").
The actual hard exclusion phrase on UTAS pages is: "This course may not be available to international students. Please see the list of distance courses (i.e. online and taken outside Australia) that are offered to international students" followed by a redirect link, AND no international fee section appears anywhere else on the page.
The current filter triggers on the first sentence alone. Need a stricter check.
Suggested fix: Make the may not be available to international students arm conditional on absence of international fee/IELTS/admission-international-section content elsewhere on the page. Pseudocode:
pythonif MAY_NOT_BE_AVAILABLE_RE.search(page_text):
    # Soft signal — only block if NO international section exists elsewhere
    if not _has_international_section(page_text):
        return DOMESTIC_ONLY
    # Otherwise course IS available internationally despite the warning
Where _has_international_section checks for: international fee structure, international entry requirements, international admission link, or <div id="tabInternational"> (the structural marker we know exists from the prompt fix).
Verification once fixed: spot-check that 93B and 93C re-scrape WITHOUT being blocked by domestic-only, and stage with international_fee values from the International tab. Currently they're being blocked PRE-extraction.
