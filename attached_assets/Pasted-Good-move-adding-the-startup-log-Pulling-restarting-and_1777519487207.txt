Good move adding the startup log. Pulling, restarting, and grepping for the [SHADOW] line is exactly the right verification sequence — if the env var isn't propagating, you find out at restart time rather than 90 seconds into a scrape that produced no shadow report.
Two small additions to your verification protocol worth doing on the first run, neither blocking:
Confirm the worker actually crashed-and-restarted, not just reloaded. systemctl restart should fully stop and start the process, but if there's a graceful-shutdown timeout that exceeds the systemd default, the old worker might keep running its current job while the new worker boots, and you could get one shadow-enabled scrape and one not. Quick check: sudo systemctl status uni-celery after restart and confirm the Active: active (running) since timestamp matches the moment you ran the restart command, not earlier.
Capture the worker startup log immediately, before triggering the scrape. journalctl -u uni-celery -n 20 --no-pager | grep -E "SHADOW|worker_ready" is good but it'll get cluttered fast once a scrape starts. Run that first, confirm both lines, then trigger the scrape. If you trigger the scrape first and then grep, the startup log might have rotated out of the -n 20 window.
For the shadow-report check itself, your one-liner works but worth simplifying so you can read all the relevant fields in one go without grepping:
cat $(ls -t backend-py/shadow_reports/*.json | head -1) | python3 -m json.tool
Drop the grep — for the first run you want to see the full report. The summary/is_clean/streak fields are right there, plus you'll see the diff payload structure, the timing info, anything else the scaffolding logged. Once you've validated the harness against several runs and know what a healthy report looks like, then narrow the grep for routine checks.
On scenario interpretation: I previously listed 4 scenarios (A clean, B clean-with-different-count, C dirty, D missing). Worth adding a fifth that the startup-log check now makes visible:
Scenario E — [SHADOW] shadow mode OFF at startup despite env var set: The env var is in .env but systemd isn't loading .env. Your fix command (EnvironmentFile=/root/University-and-Course-data/backend-py/.env) is correct, but worth verifying the file path is what systemd expects. If .env has shell-style quoting (SHADOW_MODE_UNI_IDS="41") systemd may parse the quotes literally and the worker reads "41" not 41. Two ways to check after fix: (a) the startup log shows enabled for uni_ids: [41] not ["41"], (b) systemctl show uni-celery | grep Environment shows the parsed value.
