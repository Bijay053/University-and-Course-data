Look at these log lines from the Torrens scrape:

Torrens Master of Information Technology page text: 1 year, 8 months / 12 subjects / 5 trimesters → regex output 1.7, stored as Year (correct unit by accident, see Bug 3)

Torrens Master of Business Analytics page text: 1 year, 8 months / 5 trimesters → 1.7 Year (correct)

Pattern: the regex is matching whichever number it sees first that's near a duration-ish word, and the unit handling is broken. In the 12 Year cases, it's grabbing 12 from 12 subjects and labeling it Year. The regex needs to either:

Anchor unit detection to the same numeric token (so 12 subjects never matches the duration pattern at all), OR
Default unit to null and trigger AI fallback when ambiguous, instead of defaulting to Year

The fix is to widen the sanity rule: any duration ≥ 1.5 with unit = Year AND degree_level in (diploma, advanced diploma, graduate certificate, graduate diploma) should be flagged. Those degree levels max out at ~2 years; anything higher is an extraction error.
Bug 3 — float32 round-trip in stored duration values
The Torrens table shows values like 1.7000000476837158 Year and 0.699999988079071 Year across 13 courses. That 1.7000000476837158 is the canonical signature of np.float32(1.7) cast back to Python float. Something in the duration write path is going through a 32-bit float — likely numpy, a Pydantic confloat, or a SQLAlchemy Float column that should be Numeric / DOUBLE PRECISION.
Two ways to fix:

Cheap: round(duration_value, 2) at write time. Display becomes clean, underlying type still wrong.
Right: store duration in integer months, convert to years for display. Eliminates the precision class entirely.
