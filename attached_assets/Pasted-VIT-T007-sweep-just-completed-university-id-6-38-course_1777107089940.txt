**Issue 3 (MEDIUM): Vocational course name normalization broken**

Look at the staged names:
```
Sit40521 - Certificate Iv in Kitchen Man
Sit30821 - Certificate Iii in Commercial
Sit31021 - Certificate Iii in Patisserie
Ict40120 - Certificate Iv in Information
Cpc30220 - Certificate Iii in Carpentry
```

Three problems:
- (a) AQF prefix "Sit40521" / "Ict40120" / "Cpc30220" is the course code, not the name. Should be stripped or moved to a course_code field.
- (b) Title case is wrong: "Iv" should be "IV", "Iii" should be "III". The acronym preservation logic isn't handling Roman numerals.
- (c) Some names truncated mid-word: "Kitchen Man" should be "Kitchen Management", "Commercial" should be "Commercial Cookery". Looks like a length-cap issue somewhere in the name extraction or normalization step.

Fix `course_name.py` extractor: (a) strip leading `[A-Z]{3}\d{5}` AQF code patterns, (b) preserve Roman numerals (II/III/IV/V/VI/VII/VIII/IX/X) in title casing, (c) find the truncation bug.

The non-vocational courses look fine ("Bachelor of Information Technology and Systems (BITS)", "MITS - Software Engineering Specialisation") so this only affects vocational paths.

**Issue 4 (LOW): Duration unit normalization for vocational courses**

Vocational courses display weeks instead of months/years:
```
Certificate III in Carpentry         — 104 Week
Adv Diploma of Information Tech      — 104 Week
Diploma of Information Technology    — 66 Week
Certificate IV in Information Tech   — 52 Week
```

104 weeks = 2 years, 52 weeks = 1 year. Convert weeks ≥ 52 to years (rounded), or weeks ≥ 12 to months. Bachelor and Master courses correctly show "3 Year" / "2 Year" because their pages use year units natively. This is just a unit normalization in `duration.py` — if `unit==week` and `value≥52`, convert to years.
